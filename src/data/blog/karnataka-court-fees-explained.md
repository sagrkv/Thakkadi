## The Two Types of Court Fees

Under the **Karnataka Court Fees and Suits Valuation Act, 1958**, court fees fall into two broad categories:

1. **Ad valorem fees** — calculated as a percentage of the suit value, using a slab-based system (like income tax)
2. **Fixed fees** — a flat amount regardless of suit value

Most suits for money, property, or specific performance attract ad valorem fees. Simpler matters like adoption suits, revenue court applications, and public interest matters attract fixed fees.

## The 16-Slab Ad Valorem System

Schedule I, Article 1 of the Act prescribes a progressive slab structure:

| Suit Value | Rate |
|-----------|------|
| Up to Rs. 15,000 | 2.5% |
| Rs. 15,001 – 30,000 | 3.0% |
| Rs. 30,001 – 50,000 | 3.5% |
| Rs. 50,001 – 1,00,000 | 4.0% |
| Rs. 1,00,001 – 2,00,000 | 4.5% |
| Rs. 2,00,001 – 3,00,000 | 5.0% |
| Rs. 3,00,001 – 5,00,000 | 5.5% |
| Rs. 5,00,001 – 10,00,000 | 6.0% |
| Rs. 10,00,001 – 15,00,000 | 6.5% |
| Rs. 15,00,001 – 20,00,000 | 7.0% |
| Above Rs. 20,00,000 | 7.5% |

This is **marginal** — each slab applies only to the amount within that range, just like income tax brackets.

## Special Suit Types

Not every suit uses the standard ad valorem scale. The Act has specific provisions for:

- **Declaratory suits (Section 24)** — full market value for possession, half for injunction
- **Partition suits (Section 35)** — ad valorem when title denied, fixed fees when in joint possession
- **Mortgage suits (Section 32)** — varies by type (recovery, redemption, foreclosure)
- **Injunction suits (Section 26)** — half market value when title denied
- **Probate/Succession (Article 6)** — separate tiered system capped at Rs. 30,000

## Appeal Fees

Under Section 49, the fee on a memorandum of appeal equals the fee payable on the plaint in the court of first instance. So if your original suit attracted Rs. 50,000 in court fees, the appeal fee is also Rs. 50,000.

For review applications, the fee depends on timing:
- Filed within 90 days: **half** the original plaint fee (Article 5)
- Filed after 90 days: **full** plaint fee (Article 5A)

## Refunds

If a suit is settled, dismissed, or transferred before trial, you may be entitled to a partial refund of court fees. The refund amount depends on the stage at which the case ended.
