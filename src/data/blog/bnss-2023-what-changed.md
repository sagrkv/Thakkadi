## The Transition

On **1 July 2024**, the Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita, 2023 (BNSS) replaced the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (CrPC) as the primary criminal procedural law in India. This was part of a larger overhaul that also replaced the IPC with BNS and the Indian Evidence Act with BSA.

For practitioners working with post-judgment remedies, the key question is: **which sections of CrPC map to which sections of BNSS?**

## Key Section Mapping

| Topic | CrPC Section | BNSS Section | What Changed |
|-------|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| Appeals from convictions | 374 | **415** | Added sub-section (4): appeals against BNS ss. 64-71 must be disposed within 6 months |
| Appeal against HC conviction reversal | 379 | **420** | Substantively identical |
| Calling for records (revision) | 397 | **438** | Clarified that Executive Magistrates are also "inferior" for revision purposes |
| HC's powers of revision | 401 | **442** | Added sub-section (5): HC can treat a mistaken revision application as an appeal |
| Inherent powers of HC | 482 | **528** | Identical — "Sanhita" replaces "Code" |

## What Stays the Same

The core procedural framework hasn't changed dramatically for post-judgment matters:

- **Limitation periods remain the same** — the Limitation Act, 1963 governs these independently
- **Appeal hierarchy unchanged** — Magistrate → Sessions → High Court → Supreme Court
- **SLP provisions unchanged** — Article 136 of the Constitution still governs
- **Review provisions** — SC Rules 2013 continue to apply

## What's New in BNSS

A few notable additions relevant to practitioners:

1. **Mandatory timelines** — Section 415(4) requires disposal of certain appeals within 6 months
2. **Revision flexibility** — Section 442(5) allows HC to convert a wrongly-filed revision into an appeal
3. **Electronic proceedings** — BNSS adds provisions for electronic filing and video conferencing throughout
4. **Zero FIR** — FIR can be filed at any police station regardless of jurisdiction

## Transitional Provisions

Cases filed **before 1 July 2024** continue under CrPC. Cases filed after that date fall under BNSS. This means practitioners may need to reference both laws for some time.

Our [Limitation Calculator](/limitation-calculator) already cites both — each criminal procedure reference shows the BNSS section with the former CrPC section noted. For example: "Section 415, BNSS 2023 (formerly Section 374, CrPC)".
